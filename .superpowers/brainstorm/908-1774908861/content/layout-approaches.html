3 approaches to the single-page layout

Same content, different structure and emphasis. Which flow feels right?

① HERO — Bold headline + 3 service tags + CTA
② SERVICES — 3 cards: Technical · Asset Mgmt · Compliance
③ WHY RPW — 3–4 differentiator stats/statements
④ ABOUT RICHARD — Photo + bio + credentials
⑤ CONTACT — Form + email

A — Classic Consultancy

Services lead, then build trust, then reveal Richard, then convert. Logical top-down journey. Works well when the services are the primary draw.

Pros

  • Familiar, expected structure
  • Services immediately visible

Cons

  • Richard appears late in scroll
  • Less personal feel
① HERO — Richard's photo + headline + CTA
② ABOUT RICHARD — Credentials, background, approach
③ SERVICES — 3 pillars expanded with bullet lists
④ HOW WE WORK — Process / engagement model
⑤ CONTACT — Form + email

B — Authority-Led

Richard is the brand — his face and credentials appear immediately. Suits boutique consultancies where trust in the individual matters most.

Pros

  • Builds personal trust fast
  • Differentiates from big firms

Cons

  • Needs good headshot to land
  • Services deeper in scroll
① HERO — Headline + subline + dual CTA
② PROBLEMS WE SOLVE — 3 pain point statements
③ SERVICES — 3 pillars with expandable detail
④ ABOUT RICHARD — Photo + bio + credentials
⑤ CONTACT — Form + email

C — Problem-First

Opens with the client's pain points before showing services. Makes visitors feel understood before pitching. Strong for mixed audiences — each group sees their problem named.

Pros

  • Resonates with all 3 audience types
  • Strongest conversion logic

Cons

  • Needs sharp copywriting
  • Slightly longer to reach services
MY RECOMMENDATION

Layout C — with the mixed audience (FM managers, property owners, contractors), leading with pain points ensures every visitor type immediately feels "this is for me" before the services pitch.
